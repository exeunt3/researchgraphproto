# Open Machine — Research Paradigm Tree

> A research and practice collective synthesizing immanent philosophy, protocol underground history,
> and emergent technology — operating across all three movements simultaneously.

---

## Open Machine

Occupies the intersection of critique, archive, and construction: diagnosing the mechanisms of
control, excavating the protocol underground as an empirical archive, and building the overgrounding
infrastructure that makes underground values transmissible without standardizing them.

**Core concepts:** Control · Protocol Underground · Overgrounding · Extitution · Open Protocols · Substrate

---

## Movement I — The Critique of Control

Control is the organizational mode — inherited from Emanationist cosmology — that constitutes power
as emanating from an interior source, mystifying the relational and networked nature of all power.
A Control organization is composed of a protocol and a ban: the mystified withholding of access to
the relational elements that animate it.

**Core concepts:** Control · Emanationism · Enclosure · Flat Ontology · Field Ontology

### ├── Cognitive Warfare
Enclosure of attention and sensory life — surveillance capitalism, algorithmic media, AI narrative
compression, psychological targeting architectures, and aesthetic conditioning as governance.
Platform monopolies are the contemporary enclosers of the cognitive commons, operating the same
logic as the 17th-century enclosure of common lands.
- Concepts: Enclosure · Aesthetics · Imaginal Sovereignty · Haptic Vision · d/acc

### ├── The Institutional Mind
Control organizations function through an administrative elite analogous to a priest class —
shuffling papers and metaphysical presumptions, mystifying the relational power that actually
animates their protocols. Institutional mind-blindness, bureaucratic epistemology, legibility
regimes, and process fascism are its contemporary faces.
- Concepts: Control · Enclosure · Extitutional Space · Substrate · Mutualism

### ├── Ontologies of Control
Emanationism: the cosmological logic according to which all power flows downward from a unitary
First Principle — God, Sovereign, Brand, Blood, Nation. Against it: the "network heresy" — powers
of creation are democratized and relationalized, arising from the interaction of parts.
- Concepts: Emanationism · Flat Ontology · Assemblage · Field Ontology · The Virtual

### ├── Political Economy of Control
The Moloch meme describes coordination failures — multipolar traps where rational actors
collectively produce outcomes none desire. But the frame overextends: it cannot address problems
whose roots are axiomatic, pre-rational ontological assumptions that define the game board and
the players. Drawing on Afropessimist tradition (Patterson, Hartman, Moten, Sexton).
- Concepts: Moloch · Open-Ended Intelligence · Mutualism · Undercapital · Flat Ontology

### └── Cognitive Infrastructure Capture
Enclosure of the internet by platform monopolies, of money by financialized institutions, of
knowledge by NDAs and classification, and of aesthetic life by administrative space design.
Ethereum as a technology of de-enclosure: "permissive abundance of network wealth" against
"premises of ownership of that which cannot be owned."
- Concepts: Enclosure · d/acc · Imaginal Sovereignty · Ethereum Localism · Open Protocols

## Movement II — Investigation of the Archive

Excavating protocol undergrounds as empirical archives. Loose, informal communities tethered by
open protocols — CA LSD underground, UK Free Party movement, Bay Area S&M scene, Hong Kong 2019 —
solved under legal pressure the coordination problems institutions claim are unsolvable.

**Core concepts:** Protocol Underground · Overgrounding · Extitution · Consent Culture · High Agency · Substrate

### ├── The Protocol Underground
Loose, informal communities tethered together only by open protocols, in which extitutional dynamics
are dominant. Three structural characteristics: high agency, participatory aesthetics, ontological
creativity. Historical examples: California LSD scene · UK Free Party Movement · Bay Area S&M scene
· Hong Kong 2019.
- Concepts: Protocol Underground · Overgrounding · Consent Culture · High Agency · Aesthetics · Extitution · Extitutional Space

### ├── Underground Epistemic Cultures
Underground cultures as avant-gardes of consent culture — not as legal formality but as living,
continuous, participatory practice. The S&M scene's "two squeezes" technique formalizes ongoing
consent without breaking the mood. High agency as mutual assumption: distributed vigilance replacing
institutional guarantees.
- Concepts: Consent Culture · High Agency · Substrate · Haptic Vision · Aesthetics · Extitutional Space

### ├── Economies of the Virtual
Undercapital: the expanse of economic value visible once virtual capital is recognized as a ninth
form alongside Gregory Landua's eight forms. The virtual is "real but not actual" — the generative
field of potentiality that animates material processes without being directly quantifiable.
- Concepts: Undercapital · The Virtual · Mutualism · Undualing · Field Ontology · Flat Ontology

### ├── The Immanent Tradition
Lewis Mumford's neotechnic epoch — electricity and alloy complex — as historical analogue for the
current protocol moment. Process philosophy (Whitehead, Bergson, Deleuze) · Feminist new
materialisms (Barad, Haraway, Braidotti) · Neurophenomenology (Varela, Maturana) ·
The Undercommons (Moten, Harney).
- Concepts: Neotechnics · Autopoiesis · Assemblage · Field Ontology · The Virtual · d/acc

### ├── Ecological Intelligence Traditions
Cooperation, not competition, is the primary driver of evolutionary complexity. Indigenous ecological
knowledge · Systems ecology · Biosemiotics (von Uexküll: Umwelt) · Morphogenetic biology (Levin:
basal cognition) · Autopoiesis (Varela, Maturana) · Symbiogenesis (Margulis).
- Concepts: Autopoiesis · Distributed Cognition · Umwelt · Noöpunk · Assemblage · Open-Ended Intelligence

### └── Underground Governance Experiments
Ethereum Localism: a strategy of reciprocal mimesis between open internet protocols and the open
pragmatist cultures of cities and bioregions. Temporary autonomous zones · Commons governance
(Ostrom) · Council-DAOs · Cosmo-local governance. Cities as anticapture devices.
- Concepts: Ethereum Localism · DAO · Extitution · Mutualism · d/acc · Extitutional Space · Substrate

## Movement III — The Construction of Immanent Technology

Overgrounding the underground at scale: making underground practices repeatable, memeable, and
formally legible — without standardizing or institutionalizing them. Distributed Ledger Technologies
as a chance to do the impossible: scale the underground, embolden communities everywhere with
participatory agency over the aesthetic environments they inhabit.

**Core concepts:** Overgrounding · Formalization Without Standardization · Open Protocols · Extitution · Memeticism

### ├── Immanent Technology Criteria
Formalization without standardization: achieving shared protocols for mutual coherence without
imposing hegemonic claims or institutional authority. Memeticism: actively encouraging divergent
reproduction and mutation. Distribute agency · Enable aesthetic co-creation · Affirm consent ·
Provoke processual thinking · Design for open empiricism.
- Concepts: Formalization Without Standardization · Memeticism · Open Protocols · Consent Culture · High Agency · Aesthetics

### ├── Cognitive Sovereignty
High agency at scale: the mutual assumption that participants actively shape situations,
infrastructures, and outcomes — not delegating material safety and vigilance to third parties.
Self-owned cognitive infrastructure · Personal data autonomy · Local AI · Anti-manipulation
architectures. Ethereum's non-coercive infrastructure as scaling mechanism for high-agency culture.
- Concepts: High Agency · Imaginal Sovereignty · d/acc · Extitutional Space · Consent Culture · Substrate

### ├── Open Protocol Design
Memetic, empirical, anti-capture protocol design: protocols that circulate freely through the
social field, crystallizing into maximally versatile and practical forms. The S&M scene's handbooks
formalize safety without standardizing aesthetics. FOSS formalizes sharing norms without
standardizing what software should do. Forkability as health indicator and anti-capture mechanism.
- Concepts: Open Protocols · Formalization Without Standardization · Memeticism · DAO · Extitutional Space · Overgrounding

### ├── Cognitive Ecology
Noöpunk: the universe as a landscape of nested and entangled cognitive assemblages — minds
distributed across organisms, ecosystems, dynamic material processes, and socio-technical networks.
Open-Ended Intelligence (Weinbaum/Veitas): distributed heterogeneous agents achieving progressively
higher coordination through reciprocal determination — identity as metastable and provisional.
- Concepts: Noöpunk · Open-Ended Intelligence · Distributed Cognition · Umwelt · Autopoiesis · Assemblage · Flat Ontology

### ├── Extitutional Infrastructure
Extitutions: small, ephemeral organizational entities that use institutional legibility to secure,
protect, and formalize the open empiricism of protocol undergrounds. Substrate: the permissionless
social field that animates and checks organizational life. The walkaway test: can an organization
be deliberately abandoned without loss to the protocol?
Historical: Brotherhood of Eternal Love · Spiral Tribe · Society of Janus.
- Concepts: Extitution · Substrate · Egregore · Extitutional Space · DAO · Field Ontology · Overgrounding

### ├── Process-Based Economic Design
Undercapital as design practice: building economic systems that treat the virtual as a primary
productive force. Intersubjective contextual monies encoding context and relation rather than
abstracting from them. Intent-centric architectures (vagabond technology) organized around declared
intent. "The felt sense of possibility is not epiphenomenal but causal."
- Concepts: Undercapital · The Virtual · Undualing · Mutualism · Field Ontology · Ethereum Localism · Assemblage

### └── Posthuman Technogenesis
Posthumanism critiques the humanist insistence on human cognitive supremacy. Ethereum as posthuman
infrastructure: permissionless, ontologically agnostic, capable of hosting zoological, ecological,
and exotic cognitive agents. Egregores — collective psychic entities generated by focused attention
— as candidates for empirical investigation and economic formalization.
- Concepts: Posthumanism · Egregore · Assemblage · Flat Ontology · Noöpunk · Open-Ended Intelligence · DAO

## Concept Glossary

### Control
The organizational mode that constitutes power as emanating from an interior source (a brand, a
divine right, blood or soil), thereby mystifying the relational and networked nature of all power.
A Control organization is composed of a protocol and a ban — the mystified withholding of access
to the relational elements that animate it.

### Emanationism
The cosmological and organizational logic according to which all power flows downward from a unitary
First Principle — God, Sovereign, Brand, Nation. Against emanationism: the network heresy — powers
of creation arise from the interaction of parts, not from any interior source.

### Enclosure
The historical and ongoing capture of common lands, shared knowledge, open protocols, and relational
power by centralized powers. The 17th–19th century English enclosures correspond to the modern
enclosure of the internet (platform monopolies), of money (financialized institutions), and of
aesthetic life.

### Moloch
A meme from web3 circles describing coordination failures — multipolar traps where rational actors
collectively produce outcomes none desire. Treated critically: the Moloch frame overextends, unable
to address problems whose roots are axiomatic — pre-rational ontological violence that defines the
game board and the players themselves.

### Aesthetics
Following Stiegler's use of aisthēsis: the question of feeling and sensibility in general — the
fundamental question of politics. Power "is nothing less than this matter of feeling." Protocol
undergrounds are zones of aesthetic self-determination against administrative capture.

### Flat Ontology
The philosophical conviction that all categories of being are equally real — no hierarchy of
substance makes some entities more real or deserving of consideration than others. Associated with
Latour's actor-network theory and DeLanda's neo-materialism. For Ethereum, flat ontology is native.

### Imaginal Sovereignty
The practice of constructing novel relations with entities at the edge of institutional legibility —
beings and forces that exist at the threshold of the administrative real. Ethereum proposed as a
potential regime of imaginal sovereignty: an open State with infrastructure that facilitates creative
legibility on the threshold of the real.

### Field Ontology
An ontological disposition in which attention and operative preeminence shift according to the
contingency of the encounter, rather than being fixed by categorical hierarchies. Favors
interrelation, intersubjectivity, and codeterminacy over static discrete objects.

### Assemblage
From Deleuze and Guattari: heterogeneous combinations of elements — bodies, tools, affects, signs,
organisms — that function together to produce emergent capacities neither element possesses alone.
Assemblages are not totalities; their interactive capacities are open and inexhaustible.

### Mutualism
Encounter-based, non-coercive political orientation grounded in free sharing between peers without
basis in centralized institutions. The extitutional form of cooperative behavior — spontaneous
association and alliance through free encounter in extitutional space.

### Open-Ended Intelligence
Weinbaum and Veitas (2015): "a distributed population of interacting heterogeneous agents achieves
progressively higher levels of coordination" through reciprocal determination — not toward a
predetermined goal but by bringing forth new integrated assemblages. Identity as metastable and
provisional.

### Protocol Underground
Loose, informal communities tethered together only by open protocols, in which extitutional dynamics
are dominant. Protocol undergrounds emerge wherever aesthetic practices face legal prohibition —
they protocolize, becoming more pluralistic and resistant to institutional capture.

### Overgrounding
Making underground practices repeatable, memeable, and formally legible — without standardizing or
institutionalizing them. A horizontal alternative to "to scale is to die": distributing the protocols
rather than the organization.

### Consent Culture
The rigorous culture of ongoing, explicit, situated agreement that defines protocol undergrounds.
The S&M scene's "two squeezes" technique is emblematic: proactive consent maintained throughout a
session without breaking the mood.

### High Agency
The mutual assumption that participants actively shape situations, infrastructures, and outcomes
rather than delegating basic safety and vigilance to third parties. High agency is not merely
individual self-determination but a shared expectation that generates a different social atmosphere.

### Extitution
A small, often ephemeral organizational entity that uses institutional legibility to secure, protect,
and formalize the open empiricism of protocol undergrounds. Often adopts a trickster character —
deliberately disingenuous, playful use of institutional structures while the extitutional networks
it serves remain in productive illegibility.

### Substrate
A permissionless, spontaneous, and shifting assemblage of identity affiliation that functions as a
locus of reciprocity — the informal social field that animates, sustains, and checks organizational
life. Substrates have no walls and continuously build the ground they stand on.

### Undercapital
The expanse of economic value visible once virtual capital is recognized as a ninth form alongside
Gregory Landua's eight forms. Designates practical assets accumulated by underground communities
who discovered that nondual experiences of possibility are a core logistical resource.

### The Virtual
From Bergson and Deleuze: the real and materially embedded trace of potential that exists alongside
or within the world of proper things. "Real but not actual" — it does not disappear when expressed,
but withdraws back into itself, simultaneously the just-past of an effect and the to-come of the next.

### Undualing
From Austin Wade Smith: the practice of reanimating plural lifeworlds against forces of
singularization — thickening "the cast of characters which create value and sustain planetary
livelihood." Moving from the dualism at the heart of modernity back into a multitude of ontological
regimes.

### Neotechnics
Lewis Mumford's term for the third epoch of technological revolution — electricity and alloy complex
— carrying the potential for decentralized production and restoration of autonomy. "We are the
neotechnics": decentralized ledger technology is continuous with a longer history of distributed
production infrastructure.

### Autopoiesis
Varela and Maturana: the capacity of a system to produce and maintain itself — the operational
definition of life as cognition. Deliberately substrate-agnostic: asks not what the system is made
of but whether it maintains internal coherence through ongoing self-production.

### Distributed Cognition
Hutchins: cognition is not the exclusive property of individual minds but is distributed across
socio-technical systems, material environments, and social networks. From this: a cascade of radical
empirical claims — Margulis, Shapiro, Levin on basal cognition in non-neural systems.

### Umwelt
Von Uexküll: the species-specific subjective perceptual world of an organism — the "self-world"
constituted by the signals an organism can detect and the actions it can take. The "umwelt gap"
is the practical challenge of building economic and governance systems that can engage diverse
intelligences.

### Noöpunk
An aesthetic movement and design philosophy seeing the universe as nested and entangled cognitive
assemblages. Also a political and economic program: nonhuman minds will remain marginalized until
they can participate economically. Contests the "chauvinism of the human mind" in technological
imagination.

### Ethereum Localism
A strategy of reciprocal mimesis between open internet protocols and the open pragmatist cultures
of cities and bioregions. Both defined not by what they are but by what they can do — both share
immediacy, pirate empiricism, and dread of coercive authority.

### DAO
A cryptoeconomic organizational form characterized by its embeddedness in substrate communities,
permissionless membership, and structural incapacity to maintain the "ban." Design features
(ragequit, forks, audits, transparent governance) foreclose ideological enclosure.

### Open Protocols
Sociotechnical knowledge sets that circulate freely through the social field, crystallizing before
diverse publics into maximally versatile and practical forms. Two defining features: memeticism
(actively encouraging reproduction) and open-ended empiricism (continuously revised by practical
outcomes).

### Formalization Without Standardization
Achieving shared protocols for mutual coherence without imposing hegemonic claims or institutional
authority. Formalization: shared standards sufficient for coordination without ideological
uniformity. Standardization: the hegemonic claim that one form is correct.

### Memeticism
The quality of actively encouraging its own reproduction and divergent application — one of the two
defining features of open protocols. Contrasted with institutional anti-memeticism: NDAs, industrial
secrets, proprietary knowledge, brand enclosure.

### Extitutional Space
The ontologically flat and pluralistic terrain outside institutional enclosures — not an absence but
a generative terrain of emergent social possibility. Three characteristic practices: horizontal
scaling, field ontology, open protocolization.

### Egregore
A collective psychic or agential entity generated by the focused attention, shared belief, or
coordinated activity of a group. Protocol undergrounds generate scene egregores — "crowd
consciousnesses, agential vibes and colors out of space" — whose existence is empirically available
but dismissed by institutional reality regimes.

### Posthumanism
Critiques the humanist insistence on human cognitive supremacy in light of cybernetic and
systems-theoretic discoveries. "In the posthuman view, conscious agency has never been 'in control.'
In fact, the very illusion of control bespeaks a fundamental ignorance about the nature of the
emergent processes through which consciousness, the organism, and the environment are constituted."

### Haptic Vision
From Deleuze and Guattari: haptic vision is the tactile sense applied to sight — perceiving with
reciprocity, putting oneself at stake, crossing the wall of fear to inhabit the earth. Contrasted
with optic vision: divisive, hierarchical, captive to the Western gaze.

### d/acc
Vitalik Buterin's philosophy of "defensive acceleration" or "decentralized acceleration" — orienting
technology development toward distributed, defensive infrastructure over concentrated capabilities.
Enriched by Mumford's neotechnic frame. Decentralization applies "in cyberspace and in soil."
